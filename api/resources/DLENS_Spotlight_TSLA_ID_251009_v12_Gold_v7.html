Short Summary (plain language)

Tesla is building the full stack for the future of transport and energy. It makes electric cars and trucks, runs fast, cost‑down factories that keep removing parts, ships software updates that add features, trains Full Self‑Driving (FSD) so cars can work as robotaxis, sells big batteries for homes and the grid, and is building Optimus, a general‑purpose robot. Inside the car, an AI helper like Grok points to a smarter, safer in‑car experience. These parts help each other and grow stronger together.

Why this is big: If FSD works at scale, driving can fade away and cars can earn money while you sleep. Energy storage adds steady profits. Optimus could open a market even larger than cars. This is a platform, not a single product.

Expanded Summary:

What Tesla does now — designs and builds EVs (sedans, SUVs, pickup, semi), runs high‑volume factories that push cost and speed, and operates an OTA software stack.
